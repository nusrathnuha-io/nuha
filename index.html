Register

Helping is often described as connecting private troubles with public issues. The helper’s training and perspective recognizes the individual but is forced to look beyond the individual and consider the larger societal issue. The family caring for an older adult needs help with coping as a family, but why does society not provide better resources to help such families? What can we do as neighbors and community members to help our aging members? Because helpers feel this sense of connectedness, they are quite adept at seeing people’s strengths and identifying people as resources for each other and society at large. For example, where someone sees corruption, a helper may see differences in values and opinions; where someone would like to condemn a helper would be more likely to educate; and where someone would fight, a helper might mediate.
